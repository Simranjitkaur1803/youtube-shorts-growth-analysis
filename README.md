# YouTube Shorts Growth Analysis

## Objective
To identify the key factors influencing the growth and virality of YouTube Shorts using engagement, content characteristics, and time-based performance.

## Dataset Overview
- Dataset contains 799 YouTube Shorts videos
- Includes views, likes, comments, age in days, engagement rate, views per day, and description length
- Views per day was used as the main growth metric

## Analysis

### 1. Engagement vs Growth Velocity
The scatter plot showed a weak relationship between engagement rate and views per day. Several videos achieved high growth despite relatively low engagement, suggesting that engagement alone is not a strong predictor of virality.

![Engagement vs Growth](engagement_vs_growth.png)

### 2. Age vs Growth Velocity
The second scatter plot showed a strong negative relationship between age and views per day. Newer videos had much higher daily growth, while older videos showed significantly lower growth.

![Age vs Growth](age_vs_growth.png)

### 3. Content Patterns in High-Performing Videos
A review of top-performing Shorts showed common content themes such as:
- humor
- relatable daily-life situations
- family interactions
- emotional or curiosity-driven titles

## Key Insights
- Early days after publishing are critical for growth
- Engagement alone does not guarantee high growth
- Recency is a stronger driver of views per day
- Viral Shorts often use simple, relatable, and curiosity-driven titles

## Recommendations
- Focus on creating strong momentum in the first few days after publishing
- Use simple and curiosity-based titles
- Create content around relatable and emotionally engaging themes
- Do not rely only on engagement metrics to judge growth potential
